### 1. The "Label" Standard
In business analytics, we avoid subjective terms like "Child" or "Senior" (which implies retirement age). Instead, we use **Life-Stage** or **Generational** descriptors.

* **Instead of "Child":** Use **"Entry Level"** or **"<18"**. (Most companies don't hire under 18, so this bin is often unnecessary unless you have an apprenticeship program).
* **Instead of "Young Adult":** Use **"Early Career"** or **"Millennial/Gen Z"**.
* **Instead of "Senior":** Use **"Late Career"** or **"50+"**.

---

### 2. Business Impact: Why these specific bins?



* **Under 25:** High attrition is expected here (grad school, traveling, first-job exploration).
* **26–35:** This is the **"Critical Retention Zone."** These employees are often your highest-potential individual contributors. If they leave, it's usually for a "Title Jump" or "Salary Hike" at a competitor.
* **36–45:** Attrition here is often linked to **Work-Life Balance** (childcare, mortgage) or **Leadership Plateaus**.
* **46–55:** These are your **Institutional Knowledge Holders**. Attrition here is expensive because they are hard to replace.
* **55+:** Attrition here is usually **Retirement-driven** and is often planned/expected.
