### 1. The Purpose: Measuring Career "Alpha"
In business, "Alpha" refers to outperformance. **Promotion Velocity** identifies your "High Potentials" (HiPos)—the people the company is moving up the ladder faster than average.

* **High Velocity:** A "Rising Star." They get promoted every 1.5 to 2 years.
* **Low Velocity:** A "Steady Contributor." They might be great at their job, but they are moving slowly or have reached their "ceiling."

---

### 2. Impact on Attrition Hypothesis
This feature is critical for testing the **"Expectation Gap" Hypothesis**:

* **The "Star" Risk:** Surprisingly, people with **very high velocity** can be attrition risks. If they are used to being promoted every 18 months and they hit a 24-month gap, they feel the company is failing them and will look for a "Title Jump" elsewhere.
* **The "Ignored High Performer" Hypothesis:** If an employee has a high **Performance Rating** but low **Promotion Velocity**, they are your #1 attrition risk. They are doing the work but not getting the title/reward.
* **The "Leadership Pipeline" Check:** It helps consultants see if certain departments (like Sales vs. R&D) have "clogged" pipelines where nobody is moving up, leading to mass exits.

---

### 3. Brief Explanation for Understanding
Think of it like an **Acceleration Pedal**.
> "If two employees have been here for 6 years, but one has had 3 promotions and the other has had 0, their 'velocity' is completely different. The one with 0 promotions is likely looking at the exit door, while the one with 3 is likely highly engaged—unless their acceleration suddenly stops."
