### 1. The Purpose: Measuring "Career Velocity"
The **Role Stagnation Ratio** (Years in Current Role / Years at Company) tells you how much of an employee’s total time at your company has been spent in the exact same seat.

* **Ratio near 1.0:** The employee is in the same role they started in. If they’ve been there 5 years, they haven’t moved an inch.
* **Ratio near 0.1:** The employee moves to new roles frequently. They are being cross-trained or promoted.



---

### 2. Impact on Attrition Hypothesis
This feature allows you to test the **"Glass Ceiling" Hypothesis**. High-performing employees don't just want a paycheck; they want a path.

* **The "Boredom" Threshold:** From a business perspective, if the ratio is above **0.7 (70%)** for an employee who has been with the company for more than 3 years, they are a high flight risk. They likely feel their career has plateaued.
* **The "Quiet Quitting" Indicator:** Employees with high stagnation often stop putting in extra effort because they realize it doesn't lead to a new role.
* **Segmenting the Risk:** A high ratio is **normal** for a "Senior Executive" who has reached the top, but it is a **red flag** for a "Junior" or "Mid-level" employee who should be climbing.

---

### 3. Brief Explanation for Understanding
Think of it like a **"Freshness Date"** for an employee's motivation. 
> "If I have worked here for 10 years and have been in the same role for 9 of them, my skills are likely stagnating, and I am probably looking at LinkedIn daily to see who will give me a new challenge."

---

### 4. The Expert "Consultant" Insight
Once you've created this, don't just look at the ratio alone. Combine it with **Performance Rating**.

* **High Performance + High Stagnation = DANGER.** These are your best people who feel ignored. They will be recruited by competitors easily.
* **Low Performance + High Stagnation = CULPABILITY.** These employees might be "settling in" and are unlikely to leave on their own, but they might be dragging down team productivity.
